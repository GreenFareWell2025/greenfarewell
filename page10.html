About Mango

The mango (*Mangifera indica*), also known as the "King of Fruits," is a tropical fruit cherished for its sweet taste, juicy texture, and vibrant aroma.

Scientific Name

Species: *Mangifera indica*

Family: Anacardiaceae

Varieties

  • Alphonso: Sweet and aromatic, popular in India.
  • Dasheri: Fiberless and widely consumed.
  • Tommy Atkins: Known for long shelf life.
  • Keitt: Mild-flavored and large in size.
  • Haden: Juicy with fibrous pulp.

Nutritional Value (Per 100g)

  • Calories: 60 kcal
  • Carbohydrates: 15 g
  • Sugars: 14 g
  • Dietary Fiber: 1.6 g
  • Vitamin C: 44% of the daily recommended intake
  • Vitamin A: 21% of the daily recommended intake
  • Potassium: 168 mg

Health Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants like beta-carotene and mangiferin.
  • Boosts immunity with high vitamin C content.
  • Aids digestion with amylase enzymes.
  • Improves skin health by reducing blemishes.
  • Promotes heart health with potassium content.

Culinary Uses

  • Eaten fresh or blended into juices, smoothies, and desserts.
  • Used in traditional dishes like pickles, chutneys, and aamras.
  • Raw mango is used in savory dishes like salads and curries.

Cultural and Symbolic Importance

  • Mango leaves are used in Hindu rituals and decorations.
  • The mango is the national fruit of India.
  • Historically exchanged as a token of friendship and goodwill.

Growing Conditions

  • Climate: Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
  • Soil: Well-drained, loamy soil with a pH range of 5.5–7.5.
  • Sunlight: Requires full sun for optimal growth.
  • Watering: Needs regular watering but avoid waterlogging.
  • Care Tips: Prune trees to maintain shape and remove dead branches.

Interesting Facts

  • Mangoes have been cultivated for over 4,000 years.
  • India is the largest producer of mangoes globally.
  • A single mango tree can live for more than 100 years.
  • Mangoes are part of the cashew family (*Anacardiaceae*).
