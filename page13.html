  • Scientific Name: Ocimum tenuiflorum
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Common Names: Black Tulsi, Krishna Tulsi, Shyama Tulsi
  • Plant Type: Aromatic herb, grows up to 30–60 cm tall.

Physical Characteristics

  1. Plant Type:
    • A perennial aromatic herb or small shrub that grows up to 30–60 cm (12–24 inches) tall.
  2. Leaves:
    • Dark purple to blackish leaves with a slightly serrated margin.
    • Strongly aromatic and slightly spicy in flavor.
  3. Flowers:
    • Small, purplish flowers arranged in elongated racemes.
  4. Stem:
    • Purplish stem with fine hairs.
  5. Seeds:
    • Small, reddish-yellow seeds.

Cultural and Religious Significance

  1. Hinduism:
    • Considered sacred and often worshipped as an incarnation of Goddess Lakshmi.
    • Used in daily prayers and rituals, especially in Vaishnavism.
  2. Symbolism:
    • Represents purity, devotion, and spiritual awakening.
    • Believed to bring prosperity and ward off negative energies.
  3. Festivals:
    • Tulsi Vivah, a Hindu festival, celebrates the ceremonial marriage of Tulsi to Lord Vishnu.

Medicinal Uses

  1. Ayurvedic Medicine:
    • Known for its adaptogenic, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties.
    • Used to treat respiratory disorders, fever, and skin conditions.
  2. Immunity Booster:
    • Helps strengthen the immune system and fight infections.
  3. Stress Relief:
    • Acts as a natural adaptogen, reducing stress and anxiety.
  4. Diabetes Management:
    • Helps regulate blood sugar levels.
  5. Other Benefits:
    • Treats digestive issues, headaches, and inflammation.

Growing Conditions

  • Climate: Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
  • Soil:Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with a neutral pH.
  • Sunlight: Requires full sunlight for optimal growth.
  • Watering: Moderate watering; avoid waterlogging.
  • Propagation: Grown from seeds or stem cuttings.

Interesting Facts

  • Krishna Tulsi is named after Lord Krishna due to its dark purple color, resembling his complexion.
  • It is considered more potent than other Tulsi varieties due to its higher concentration of medicinal compounds..
  • Tulsi is often planted in courtyards and gardens for its spiritual and ecological benefits..
