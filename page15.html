Description

Amla is a small, round fruit with a translucent green color and a sour, tangy taste. It has six vertical stripes and a smooth texture.


The tree is medium-sized, growing up to 8 meters tall, with feathery leaves and yellowish-green flowers.


  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Phyllanthaceae
  • Genus: Phyllanthus
  • Species: Phyllanthus emblica

Nutritional Value

  • Fiber: High dietary fiber content aids digestion.
  • Minerals: Includes calcium, iron, and phosphorus.
  • Antioxidants: Rich in polyphenols, flavonoids, and tannins.
  • Vitamin C: Contains 20 times more vitamin C than an orange.

Health Benefits

  • Boosts Immunity: The high vitamin C content strengthens the immune system and helps fight infections.
  • Improves Digestion: Amla enhances gut health, alleviates constipation, and supports the digestive process.
  • Promotes Heart Health: It helps lower cholesterol levels, regulates blood pressure, and improves overall cardiovascular health.
  • Skin and Hair Care: Amla is widely used in cosmetics for glowing skin and strong, shiny hair.
  • Anti-Aging: Its antioxidants combat free radicals, slowing down the aging process.
  • Diabetes Management: Amla helps regulate blood sugar levels and improves insulin sensitivity.

Uses

  • Culinary: Amla is used to make pickles, jams, juices, and candies. It is also consumed raw or dried.
  • Medicinal: Amla is a key ingredient in Ayurvedic formulations like Triphala and Chyawanprash.
  • Cosmetics: Extracts are used in shampoos, conditioners, and skincare products.
