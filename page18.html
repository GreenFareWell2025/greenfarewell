The Colorful Climber

"A dazzling burst of beauty that thrives in sunshine."

Event Name: GreenFarewell 2025

Description

Bougainvillea is a thorny ornamental vine, shrub, or tree. It is known for its colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. The actual flowers are small and white, surrounded by bracts in shades of pink, magenta, purple, red, orange, white, or yellow


The plant can grow up to 12 meters (39 feet) tall and is evergreen in regions with year-round rainfall or deciduous in areas with dry seasons.


  • Scientific Name: Bougainvillea (Genus).
  • Family: Nyctaginaceae (four-o'clock family).
  • Species: There are between 4 and 22 species, including Bougainvillea spectabilis, Bougainvillea glabra, and Bougainvillea peruviana

Origin and History

  • Native to South America, particularly Brazil, Bolivia, Paraguay, Peru, and Argentina.
  • Named after French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville, who documented the plant during his expeditions.

Growth and Care

  • Climate: Bougainvillea thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.
  • Soil: It adapts to various soil types but prefers well-drained soil.
  • Watering: Drought-tolerant, it requires minimal watering once established.
  • Pruning: Regular pruning helps maintain its shape and encourages blooming.

Uses

  • Bougainvillea is widely used for landscaping, as it can climb walls, fences, and trellises. It is also grown in pots and shaped into bonsai.
  • Its vibrant bracts make it a popular choice for decorative purposes in gardens and public spaces.

Fun Facts

  • Bougainvillea glabra is often called "paper flower" due to its thin, papery bracts.
  • The plant's resilience and adaptability make it a favorite among gardeners worldwide.
