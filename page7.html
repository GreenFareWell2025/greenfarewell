About the Peepal Tree

The Peepal Tree, scientifically known as Ficus religiosa, is native to the Indian subcontinent and holds immense cultural, ecological, and spiritual significance. Renowned for its longevity, the Peepal tree can live for over 1,000 years.

  • Scientific Name: Ficus religiosa
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Common Names: Sacred Fig, Ashvattha Tree, Bodhi Tree
  • Size: Can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Cultural and Religious Significance

The Peepal tree is deeply revered in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism:

  • In Hinduism, the tree represents Lord Vishnu and symbolizes life and fertility.
  • It is believed that Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under this tree, making it sacred in Buddhism.
  • Jains regard it as a symbol of meditation and spirituality.
  • It is often referred to as the "Tree of Life" in Indian mythology.

Ecological and Medicinal Benefits

  • Releases oxygen day and night, making it vital for air purification.
  • Acts as a natural habitat for birds, insects, and animals.
  • Used in Ayurvedic remedies to treat ailments like asthma, diabetes, and skin disorders.

Interesting Facts

  • Some Peepal trees are over 2,000 years old.
  • It is mentioned in the Bhagavad Gita as the "Ashvattha tree."
  • The Peepal tree is often planted near temples and sacred sites in India.
