About the Night-Flowering Jasmine

Known as the **Tree of Sorrow** (*Nyctanthes arbor-tristis*), this fragrant plant is native to South Asia and Southeast Asia. Its delicate white flowers with orange centers bloom at night and fall off by morning, creating a stunning visual effect.

Planted By: Ms. Swaranjali, Ms. Nidhishree and Ms. Anushka

Scientific Name

  • Botanical Name: Nyctanthes arbor-tristis
  • Family: Oleaceae
  • Common Names: Parijat, Coral Jasmine, Harsingar
  • Plant Type: Small tree or shrub, growing up to 10 meters tall.

Cultural and Religious Significance

The Night-Flowering Jasmine holds a sacred place in Indian culture:

  • Used in religious rituals and offerings, especially during Durga Puja.
  • Associated with Hindu mythology, where its flowers are symbols of devotion and love.
  • Known as the state flower of West Bengal, India.

Medicinal and Ornamental Uses

The Night-Flowering Jasmine offers various benefits:

  • Medicinal Uses: Treats sciatica, arthritis, fever, and skin ailments. Decoctions help with bronchitis and intestinal worms.
  • Ornamental Uses: Beautifies gardens and courtyards with its fragrant blooms.
  • Dye Production: The orange corolla is used to make natural dyes for silk.

Interesting Facts

  • The name *arbor-tristis* means "sad tree" in Latin, referring to flowers that drop off by morning.
  • Despite its name, it is not a true jasmine and does not belong to the genus *Jasminum*.
  • It is known for its spiritual symbolism and cultural significance in India.
