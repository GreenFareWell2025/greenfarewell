Nature’s Sweet Delight

"A tropical treasure packed with flavor and nutrition."

Event Name: GreenFarewell 2025

Description

Chikoo is a round or oval-shaped fruit with a rough, brown skin resembling a kiwi. Its flesh is soft, grainy, and sweet, with a flavor reminiscent of brown sugar and pear.

The fruit contains 2-5 shiny black seeds and emits a pleasant malty fragrance when ripe.

Scientific Name: Genus: Manikara zapota

Event Name: GreenFarewell 2025

Origin and Cultivation

  • Native to Central America, particularly southern Mexico and the Yucatan Peninsula, Chikoo has been cultivated for centuries. It is now widely grown in India, the Philippines, and other tropical regions.
  • The Chikoo tree is evergreen and can grow up to 30 meters tall. It thrives in warm climates and produces fruit twice a year.

Nutritional Value

  • Chikoo is rich in dietary fiber, vitamins (C, A, and B-complex), and minerals like iron, copper, and calcium. It also contains antioxidants and natural sugars like fructose and sucrose.
  • A 100-gram serving provides approximately 83 kilocalories, making it a nutrient-dense fruit.

Health Benefits

  • Digestive Health: High fiber content aids in digestion and prevents constipation.
  • Energy Boost: Its natural sugars provide instant energy, making it ideal for athletes and pregnant women.
  • Immunity: Vitamin C and antioxidants strengthen the immune system.
  • Anti-Inflammatory Properties: Tannins in Chikoo help reduce inflammation and alleviate conditions like arthritis..
  • Skin and Hair: Nutrients promote healthy skin and hair growth..

Uses

  • Chikoo can be eaten raw or used in desserts, milkshakes, and smoothies. Its pulp is also used in traditional medicine to treat coughs, colds, and diarrhea.
  • The tree's latex, known as chicle, was historically used to make chewing gum

Interesting Facts

  • Chikoo is known by various names worldwide, such as Sapodilla, Naseberry, and Chicozapote.
  • The tree's wood has been used in ancient Mayan architecture, showcasing its historical significance.
