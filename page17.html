Brahmakamal: The Divine Himalayan Bloom

Brahmakamal (Saussurea obvallata) is a rare and revered flower found in the Himalayan region. It holds immense cultural, spiritual, and medicinal significance.

Botanical Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Saussurea
  • Species: Saussurea obvallata

Cultural & Mythological Significance

  • Named after Lord Brahma, the creator in Hindu mythology.
  • According to legends, Lord Brahma was born on this flower.
  • In the Ramayana, it is said that when Lakshmana was revived, gods showered Brahmakamal flowers from the heavens.
  • In the Mahabharata, Draupadi found spiritual solace upon witnessing the bloom.

Blooming Pattern

  • Blooms only once a year, during the nighttime.
  • The flowering period is July to September, after sunset.
  • Takes two hours to fully open and remains in bloom throughout the night.

Medicinal Properties

  • Used in traditional medicine for its healing properties.
  • Considered a life-giving bloom in Hindu scriptures.
