The Fragrance of Serenity

"A bloom that whispers devotion, beauty, and timeless elegance."

Event Name: GreenFarewell 2025

Botanical Overview

  • Scientific Name: Michelia Champaca / Plumeria
  • Common Names: Chafa, Champa, Frangipani, Son Champa
  • Family: Magnoliaceae (Michelia) / Apocynaceae (Plumeria)
  • Native Region: Tropical and subtropical regions, including India and Southeast Asia

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: Can grow up to 10-12 feet tall.
  • Leaves: Glossy, elongated, and dark green.
  • Flowers: Large, cup-shaped, and highly fragrant. They come in white, yellow, pink, and orange varieties.
  • Fragrance: Strong, sweet, and often used in perfumes.

Growing Conditions

  • Sunlight: Requires full sunlight for optimal growth.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained loamy or sandy soil.
  • Watering: Moderate watering; avoid overwatering.
  • Propagation: Can be grown from seeds or cuttings.

Uses & Benefits

  • Ornamental: Popular in gardens and temples due to its beauty and fragrance.
  • Medicinal: Used in Ayurveda for treating skin conditions and respiratory issues.
  • Spiritual Significance: Often associated with Hindu and Buddhist rituals.
  • Perfume Industry: Extracted for making essential oils and perfumes.

Interesting Facts

  • The Chafa flower is considered sacred and is often used in religious ceremonies.
  • It is known for its long-lasting fragrance, making it a favorite for garlands and decorations.
  • Some varieties, like Golden Champa, are highly valued for their therapeutic properties.
